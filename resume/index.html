Research

It is the duty of the journalist to compile all relevant information before beginning the writing process. For this reason, the journalist must spend the bulk of his time researching, which consists of reading, interviewing, interpreting, and following the many leads that arise. Consequently, it is crucial to understand (1) which information is relevant; (2) whether there are any unanswered questions; (3) how all of the information fits together coherently.

Negotiation

In researching for a story, it is inevitable that the journalist comes across unwilling interviewees. It is therefore imperative that the journalist develops sharp social skills. Often the only thing standing between a journalist and a good story are the reservations of the interviewee.
